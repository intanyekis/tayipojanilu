Lynn Whitfield is an American actress who first came to prominence after playing Josephine Baker in the television film, The Josephine Baker Story (1991). Additionally, she is also well known for her stellar performances in The George McKenna Story (1986), The Women of Brewster Place (1989), Heartbeat (1988-1989), Equal Justice (1991), How to Get Away with Murder (2014-2015), Greenleaf (2016-2020), etc.

    Lynn Whitfield Facts

  • She considers Ruby Dee as her role model in life.
  • Lynn celebrates her birthday on the same day as other notable Hollywood celebrities like Amber Riley, Jane Seymour, Megan Thee Stallion, Alex Borstein, Cesar Romero, Chris Farley, Christopher McDonald, Conor Oberst, and many others.
  • She starred as Halle Berry‘s mother in the television film, The Wedding (1998) even though she was only 13 years older than Berry.
  • She appeared with Martin Lawrence in the romantic comedy film, A Thin Line Between Love and Hate (1996). Whitfield, who was 42 years old at the time of filming, was 12 years older than the 30-year-old Lawrence, who played her romantic interest in the film.
  • Her breakthrough role was starring as Josephine Baker in HBO’s biographical film, The Josephine Baker Story (1991). The film depicted the life of the American-born Baker from the age of 18 to 68 years, during which she found fame as a Folies Bergère star, became a French resistance fighter during World War II, and emerged as a civil rights activist. Lynn’s phenomenal performance earned her a Golden Globe nomination as well as the Primetime Emmy Award for ‘Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie’ in 1992.
