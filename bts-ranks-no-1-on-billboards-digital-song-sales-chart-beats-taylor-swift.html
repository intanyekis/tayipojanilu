BTS Ranks No. 1 On Billboards Digital Song Sales Chart, Beats Taylor Swift

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, July 16, 2024

Last week, BTS conquered Billboard’s sales chart with their latest single in collaboration with Coldplay, a popular band from the United Kingdom. The tune blasted No.1 on the ranking of the Hot 100 and sold 127,000 copies. BTS spent 46 weeks at No.1 on the Billboard’s Digital Song Sales Chart, breaking Taylor Swift's record.

Butter also beats the record made by BTS’s own Dynamite.

BTS, a popular K-POP band, is now shattering Hollywood records. Both BTS and Taylor Swift were on the same position till the forty-fifth week. In the forty-sixth week, the band took over the No.1 position in the Digital Sales Chart. The band's latest release has not only acquired the title of best-selling album of all time but also crowned the top album sales chart in the same week, as reported by Forbes.

Taylor Swift tries to fill the spot in this frame by releasing a new album. Her recent “Folklore” is No.3 in the race. Swift and BTS have created a huge rush among pop music fans. With their huge fan base, BTS' tracks have gained popularity. However, with cut-throat competition in Hollywood, it is new for a South Korean band like BTS to be at the top in multiple spots, as reported by Kpopstarz.

BTS has topped charts six times in just one year and ranks the second-fastest by beating the Beatles, who set the record of one year and two weeks. BTS is ready to dive into an upcoming webtoon series distributed by HYBE labels in partnership with Naver Webtoon. It is based on the story of seven hunters who must fight against the creatures to protect the city from evil. The seven members of BTS have gained honor in the industry and hope to do so for a long time.
