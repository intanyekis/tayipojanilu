South Carolina residents can start process to get new 'REAL ID' WSOC TV

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, June 7, 2024

South Carolina residents can now start the process to get the new “REAL ID.”  Travelers will need either the new REAL ID license or ID, or other federally accepted identification, to pass security at airports nationwide.

[RELATED: What is Real ID; is your state compliant; what if it isnt?]

The REAL ID, or other federally accepted IDs, will also be required to enter secure federal buildings and visit military installations. Beginning Oct. 1, 2020, current South Carolina licenses and IDs will no longer be accepted.

[RELATED: What is Real ID; is your state compliant; what if it isnt?]

The South Carolina Department of Motor Vehicles (SCDMV) has released the images of the new REAL ID and non-compliant cards residents will begin seeing in the first quarter of 2018, as testing continues on the new cards.

The cards will be available to the public in 2018. For those interested in getting a REAL ID, you can begin the process now of changing your current driver’s license.

To change your card to a REAL ID, the SCDMV must have all of the following documents on file:

  • Proof of Identity (Government-issued birth certificate or valid US Passport)
  • Proof of Social Security Number
  • Two Proofs of Current, Physical SC Address
  • Proof of All Legal Name Changes

People may bring these documents to the SCDMV now.
