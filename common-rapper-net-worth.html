Early Life of Common

Common, whose real name is Lonnie Rashid Lynn Jr., was born and raised in Chicago, Illinois. He was born on March 13, 1972. Common grew up in a household where his mother was a teacher and his father was a basketball player turned youth counselor. His parents played a significant role in shaping his upbringing and nurturing his talents.

Prior to his music career, Common attended Florida A&M University for two years, studying business administration. Although he had academic pursuits, his passion for music ultimately led him to take a different path. Common made the decision to pursue his dream and embark on a career in the music industry.

Music Career of Common

Common, originally known as Common Sense, embarked on his music career in 1991 as a solo artist. He released his debut album, “Can I Borrow a Dollar?”, in 1992, marking the beginning of his journey to success. However, it was his second album, “Resurrection”, released in 1994, that propelled him to new heights, earning both commercial success and critical acclaim.

Since then, Common has released a total of 11 studio albums, each showcasing his unique style and poetic lyricism. One of his most successful albums, “Like Water for Chocolate”, solidified his position as a prominent figure in the music industry.

In recognition of his talent and contributions, Common has garnered numerous awards and accolades throughout his career. He has been honored with two BET Awards and two Grammy Awards, showcasing his immense talent and the impact of his music.

Acting and Other Ventures

In addition to his successful music career, Common has also ventured into acting and other business opportunities. He has showcased his talents on the big screen, starring in notable films such as “Smokin’ Aces,” “American Gangster,” and “Suicide Squad.”

Common has not limited himself to acting alone; he has also made a name for himself in the world of modeling and endorsements. He worked as a Gap model, representing the popular clothing brand in various campaigns. Additionally, Common has collaborated with renowned brands like Microsoft and NBA 2K8, solidifying his presence beyond the music industry.

Furthermore, Common has proven his entrepreneurial skills with the co-founding of Freedom Road Productions, a production company dedicated to creating thought-provoking and impactful content. Through this venture, Common has been able to explore his passion for storytelling and bring important narratives to life.

To add to his impressive repertoire, Common is also an accomplished author. He has written two memoirs, “One Day It’ll All Make Sense” and “Let Love Have the Last Word,” offering readers a glimpse into his life, struggles, and successes.

Activism

Common is not only a talented rapper and actor but also a passionate advocate for social change. His activism and philanthropy work have made a significant impact on various causes, demonstrating his dedication to creating a better world.

One of Common’s key areas of activism is animal rights. He is a strong supporter of PETA (People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals) and has actively collaborated with the organization to raise awareness about the mistreatment of animals and promote cruelty-free practices.

In addition to his work in animal rights, Common has founded the Common Ground Foundation. This non-profit organization aims to empower underprivileged youth through education, mentorship, and community initiatives. Through the foundation, Common has been able to make a positive impact on the lives of many young individuals, providing them with opportunities to thrive and succeed.

Moreover, Common has been involved in HIV and AIDS awareness campaigns, using his platform to educate and advocate for better healthcare, support, and prevention efforts. He has participated in rallies and events dedicated to raising awareness about these important issues, further highlighting his commitment to creating a healthier society.

Common’s activism extends beyond a single cause, as he actively supports various social issues. Through his music and performances, he has used his voice to shed light on racial inequality, environmental sustainability, and the power of community activism.

Common’s dedication to activism and philanthropy truly exemplifies the impact that individuals can have when they use their influence and resources to advocate for positive change. His work serves as an inspiration for others to join in and make a difference in the world.

White House Controversy

The Common White House controversy brought attention to the intersection of art, politics, and public perception.
